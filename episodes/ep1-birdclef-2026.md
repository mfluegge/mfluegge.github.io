# Episode 1: BirdCLEF+ 2026 - Acoustic Species Identification (but without Neural Networks)

*[Link to original Kaggle competition](https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/birdclef-2026)*

### Competition Goal & Background
Identify which bird species (from a specific set) are present in audio recordings taken in the Pantanal region. Multi-label prediction in 5s intervals. BirdCLEF is a yearly competition, always focused on detecting bird species from audio recordings, but the regions and respective bird species change every year. For this type of data, CNNs on Spectrograms are the best choice and typically make up most of the solutions to these competitions. This "clear best choice" heavily limits the number of options competiting teams get to explore and how creative they get to be, even though they produce great solutions. I'm willing to settle for clearly worse solutions in exchange for a bigger solution space that's more fun. That's why I'm banning any form of neural network, not just the convolutional kind, from my regressive episode here.

### Episode Constraints
* No Neural Networks.
* No LLM Assistants or Coding Agents of any kind.
* No peeking at any BirdCLEF+ 2026 discussion or submissions made until after I've made it through this regressive episode.
